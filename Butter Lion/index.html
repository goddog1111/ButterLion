Field Survey

Uneven Distribution of Trash Bins and Inefficient Management

Despite the high pedestrian flow around the station, trash bins are limited in number or poorly positioned. When people are unable to locate a trash bin, they may resort to littering, resulting in a messy environment.

Trash Bins Frequently Overflowing and Not Emptied Promptly

The lack of a real-time monitoring system leads to trash bins not being emptied during peak hours, causing overflow, foul odors, and unsanitary conditions.

Widespread Littering of Cigarette Butts, Causing Fire and Health Hazards

Cigarette butts are commonly seen on roads and sidewalks. These not only pollute soil and water but can also start fires or clog drainage systems, affecting public safety and the city's appearance.

Key Findings

Geographical Coverage

1,926 cleaning bins distributed across Taipei City, with concentrated presence in urban centers.

District Distribution

Highest concentration in Zhongzheng, Shilin, and Da'an districts, reflecting population density patterns.

Strategic Placement

Multiple bins at high-traffic locations such as bus stops and major intersections.

Regulatory Framework

Strict enforcement with NT$6,000 fine for household waste disposal violations.

Current Situation Analysis

Increased Pressure on Sorting Efficiency and Accuracy

A higher volume of recyclables requires more meticulous sorting. However, do sanitation workers have sufficient capacity and time to manage this demand? Without proactive updates and maintenance, the system risks declining efficiency and worsening sanitation conditions.

Increased Workload

From 2020 to 2022, the volume of waste surged by approximately 50%, yet there has been no significant increase in manpower. This has resulted in a substantial rise in the workload per person.

Proposed Solutions

Implement Smart Trash Bins and IoT Monitoring Systems These can reduce incorrect disposal, provide real-time feedback on bin capacity, and improve sorting accuracy and collection efficiency.
Enhance Education and Incentives for Recycling Incorporate NFC/QR code check-in systems to encourage the public to sort waste correctly and earn reward points.
Establish a Smart Logistics Platform (newly proposed – optional to include) Integrate waste collection data, bin status monitoring, vehicle dispatch, and workforce scheduling to optimize resource allocation.

From Challenges to Solutions

Challenges: Surge in Waste Volume & Workforce Pressure
In recent years, waste processing demands have risen significantly. Traditional labor-intensive models are no longer sufficient. Real-time monitoring and data-driven management are now essential to handle the growing load efficiently.
Solutions: Improving Sorting Accuracy, Enhancing Recycling Efficiency, and Promoting Citizen Engagement
By integrating sensors and visual feedback (such as indicator lights), citizens can be guided to dispose of waste correctly. The system is further supported by a mobile app with incentive mechanisms to encourage eco-friendly behavior.
With NFC/QR code check-ins and carbon reduction feedback, waste sorting becomes a collective action, turning sustainability into an accessible and engaging part of everyday life.

Design Concept

Preview of the Proposed Smart Trash Bin Product
Integrating real-time monitoring, sorting, and user interaction features, this smart trash bin is designed to enhance waste management efficiency and user experience in public spaces.

Interactive Smart Trash Bin Design Concept

Multi-compartment design for better waste sorting. Each compartment is clearly labeled to help users separate recyclables, general waste, and cigarette butts efficiently.
LED panel displays real-time bin capacity and sorting instructions. Users can instantly see which bins are full and receive guidance on proper sorting.
Sensors detect waste levels and transmit data to the mobile app. Sanitation teams and citizens can monitor bin status in real time, improving collection efficiency.
NFC/QR code readers for eco check-ins. Users scan their transportation cards or phones to log eco-friendly actions and earn rewards.
Audio/visual feedback for sorting accuracy. The system provides instant feedback to encourage correct disposal and reinforce positive behavior.
Track daily eco-friendly actions via mobile app. Users can view their green actions, points, and achievements, making sustainability engaging and rewarding.

Mobile Map Application Design Concept

Trash Bin Locator Integrates GPS and open data to show the nearest public trash bins and legal dumping zones. Users can plan walking routes accordingly.
Real-time Bin Capacity Display Each smart bin visually indicates its current capacity level (High/Medium/Low) using progress bars or color changes. Full bins are shown in orange, available bins in green.
Waste Sorting Assistant Users can take a photo or input the item name to receive instructions on how to properly sort and recycle it.
Carbon Reduction Info Displays estimated carbon emissions reduced through proper sorting and responsible disposal. Visual elements like growing tree animations help users understand their positive environmental impact.
Reward System Encourages proper sorting and use of public trash bins by letting users accumulate points. Points can be redeemed for discounts, small gifts, or eco badges that track progress and achievements.
